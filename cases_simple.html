Examples

How the Alzheimer's Risk Calculator Can Be Used

Example Stories

These stories show how the Alzheimer's risk calculator can be used in different situations. They are not real people, but they are based on real situations.

1
A Person with Some Memory Worries
Low Risk Example

About the Person

Age
68
Sex
Female
Memory Concerns
Some changes
Gene Type
E3/E3

Their Story

A 68-year-old woman is worried because she has been forgetting things more often. Her doctor did some tests and said her memory is normal for her age. But she is still worried because her mother had Alzheimer's. She took the blood test to see what her risk is.

Blood Test Result

Negative
This means it is very unlikely she has the brain changes of Alzheimer's disease.

Her Risk

Risk Level
Low

What This Means

This woman has a very low risk of getting Alzheimer's disease. Her doctor will look for other reasons for her memory worries.

In-Between Risk Example

About the Person

Age
74
Sex
Male
Memory Concerns
Memory problems are interfering with my life
Gene Type
E3/E4

Their Story

A 74-year-old man is having some memory problems that are starting to affect his daily life. His family is worried about him. He has a gene that puts him at a higher risk for Alzheimer's. He took the blood test to help his doctor decide what to do next.

Blood Test Result

In-between
This means it is not clear if he has the brain changes of Alzheimer's disease.

His Risk

Risk Level
High

What This Means

This man has a high risk of getting Alzheimer's disease. His doctor will probably want to do more tests to be sure.

4
An Older Person with Dementia
Very High Risk Example

About the Person

Age
82
Sex
Female
Memory Concerns
I need help with daily activities
Gene Type
E3/E4

Their Story

An 82-year-old woman has been having memory problems for a few years. She now needs help with many daily tasks. Her doctor thinks she has Alzheimer's, but wants to be sure. She took the blood test to confirm the diagnosis.

Blood Test Result

Positive
This means it is very likely she has the brain changes of Alzheimer's disease.

Her Risk

Risk Level
Very High

What This Means

This woman has a very high risk of having Alzheimer's disease. The blood test confirms what her doctor already thought. Her doctor will now be able to create a treatment plan for her.

What to do next:

Talk to your doctor about these results. They can help you understand what they mean for you and what steps you should take.
