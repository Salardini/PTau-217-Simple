About the Calculator

Information about how this tool works.

What the Calculator Does

This calculator is a tool that helps you understand your risk for Alzheimer's disease. It is not a diagnosis. It is meant to be used with your doctor to help you make decisions about your health.

The calculator uses information from a blood test, your age, and your memory concerns to estimate your risk. It also uses information about your genes, if you know it.

Where the Information Comes From

The information in this calculator comes from a study of almost 500 people who were having memory problems. The study was reviewed by the FDA, which is the part of the government that makes sure medicines and medical tests are safe and work the way they are supposed to.

We also used information from other studies about how many people get Alzheimer's at different ages, and how your genes can affect your risk.

Your Privacy

This calculator does not save any of your information. Everything you enter is only used to calculate your risk and is not stored anywhere.

What the Results Mean

The calculator will give you a risk level: low, medium, high, or very high. This is an estimate of your risk of developing Alzheimer's disease in the next few years.

A low risk means you are unlikely to have Alzheimer's. A high or very high risk means you are more likely to have Alzheimer's. A medium risk is in between.

It is important to remember that this is just an estimate. It is not a diagnosis. You should always talk to your doctor about your results.
